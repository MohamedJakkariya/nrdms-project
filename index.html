Natural Resources Data Management System

NRDMS Programme is the geospatial technology based which aims at promoting R&D for solving area specific problems. In order to help good governance and digital India programmes, NRDMS is supporting various sub programmes like Health Geographic Information Science (HGIS), Village Information System (VIS) and Revival of Village Ponds. To develop capacity in such areas, specific training course on geospatial technologies and applications are organisedfor various target groups. The progress made over last one year on geospatial policies and technical sub programmes, are as under:

Objectives :

  • To evaluate the level of soil contamination by measuring the diagnostics variables ( pH, Macronutrients, Micronutrients, Electrical Conductivity, Heavy metals) at different levels prevailing in Cauvery delta region of Trichy zone using sensors/meters.
  • To create geo-spatial database with the sensed and measured soil samples values collected during various climatic conditions
  • To perform Comparative analysis across the tested sample and samples collected from testing Lab and to classify the levels of soil contamination by fuzzy logic and association rules of spatial mining techniques for the development of spatial data mining model.
  • To map the level of contamination present in the soil of Cauvery delta region of Trichy using GIS
  • To generate the detailed report of soil analysis of the various dispersed farms in Cauvery delta region to support the farmers to find out the suitable cropping pattern.
  • To create awareness about the type of contamination levels by developing a portal to agriculturist, Environmental scientist etc.

  • State Tamil Nadu (Thiruchirappalli & Thanjavur District)

pH EC, OC, Salinity

pH is a measure of hitrogen ion concentration,a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution. The pH scale usually ranges from 0 to 14.
Electric current is the flow of electrons though a material, and electrical conductivity is the ability of electric current to flow through a material.
Organic matter (or organic material) is matter that has come from a recently living organism. It is capable of decay, or is the product of decay; or is composed of organic compounds.

Heavy Metals

Heavy metals constitute an ill-defined group of inorganic chemical hazards, and those most commonly found at contaminated sites are lead (Pb), chromium (Cr), arsenic (As), zinc (Zn), cadmium (Cd), copper (Cu), mercury (Hg), and nickel (Ni)

Macro Nutrients

Nutrients that plants require in larger amounts are called macronutrients. About half of the essential elements are considered macronutrients: carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium, and sulfur.

Micro Nutrients

Micronutrients are essential for plant growth and play an important role in balanced crop nutrition.

Fuzzy Overlay Map

The Fuzzy Overlay tool allows the analysis of the possibility of a phenomenon belonging to multiple sets in a multicriteria overlay analysis. Not only does Fuzzy Overlay determine what sets the phenomenon is possibly a member of, it also analyzes the relationships between the membership of the multiple sets.
